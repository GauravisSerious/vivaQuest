35 HIGH-PROBABILITY VIVA QUESTIONS - SHORT AND DIRECT
💻 Digital Banking & Basics
BANKING 1. Explain Digital Transformation in Banking
+
The shift from traditional "Branch-Banking" to Digital-First Banking. It involves automating core processes, using Cloud for scalability, and providing mobile-first services like UPI, Neobanking, and AI-driven customer support.
BANKING 2. Role of AI in Financial Services ⭐
+
AI revolutionizes finance through:
  • Fraud Detection: Real-time monitoring of suspicious patterns.
  • Credit Scoring: Using ML to assess risk for users without credit history.
  • Algorithmic Trading: Executing trades at speeds impossible for humans.
  • Personalized Banking: AI chatbots (NLP) and tailored investment advice.
BANKING 3. Explain Digital Money Transfer System
+
Systems that allow electronic movement of funds. Key examples:
  • RTGS: High-value, real-time settlement.
  • NEFT: Deferred settlement in batches.
  • UPI: Peer-to-peer, instant 24/7 mobile transfers using a VPA (Virtual Payment Address).
BANKING 4. Security issues in digital payments ⭐
+
Major threats include Phishing (fake links), MITM (Man-in-the-Middle) attacks where data is intercepted, SIM Swapping, and Card Not Present (CNP) fraud.
📊 Financial Statistics
STATS 5. Explain Sharpe Ratio (formula + use) ⭐
+
It measures the risk-adjusted return of an investment.
Sharpe Ratio = (Rp - Rf) / σp

Where Rp = Portfolio Return, Rf = Risk-Free Rate, σp = Std Dev (Risk).
STATS 6. Importance of Sharpe Ratio in portfolio analysis ⭐
+
It helps investors understand if their returns are due to smart investing or taking too much risk. A ratio > 1.0 is considered good; > 2.0 is very good.
STATS 7. Mean, Variance, Standard Deviation in finance
+
Mean: The average expected return.
Variance: Measures the spread of returns (risk).
Standard Deviation: The square root of variance; it is the most common measure of Volatility.
STATS 8. Correlation vs Covariance ⭐
+
Covariance: Tells you if two stocks move together (direction).
Correlation: A scaled version of covariance (between -1 and 1) that tells you how strongly they move together.
STATS 9. Skewness and Kurtosis
+
Skewness: Measures the asymmetry of returns. Most investors prefer "Positive Skew" (small chance of huge gains).
Kurtosis: Measures the "Fatness" of tails (risk of extreme rare events or 'Black Swans').
📈 Clustering (Very Important)
ML 10. Explain K-Means Algorithm ⭐
+
An Unsupervised ML algorithm that groups data points into 'K' clusters by minimizing the distance between points and their respective cluster centroid.
ML 11. Steps of K-Means clustering ⭐
+
  1. Initialize K centroids randomly.
  2. Assign each data point to the nearest centroid.
  3. Re-calculate centroids by taking the mean of all points in that cluster.
  4. Repeat until convergence (centroids stop moving).
ML 12. Applications of clustering in finance ⭐
+
  • Customer Segmentation: Grouping users by spending habits.
  • Portfolio Diversification: Finding stocks that are NOT in the same cluster to reduce risk.
  • Anomaly Detection: Identifying transactions that don't fit into any cluster.
ML 13. How to choose number of clusters ⭐
+
Common methods include the Elbow Method (plotting WCSS vs K) and the Silhouette Score (measures how well-separated clusters are).
📉 Market Sentiment
MARKET 14. Explain Markov Regime Switching Model ⭐
+
A model that assumes the market has different "states" (e.g., Bull or Bear) and uses probabilities to predict when the market will switch from one state to another.
MARKET 15. What is Market Sentiment Analysis ⭐
+
Using NLP to analyze news, social media, and reports to gauge the mood of the market (Optimistic vs. Pessimistic).
MATH 16. Bayesian Reasoning in finance ⭐
+
A statistical method where you update the probability of a hypothesis (e.g., "The market will crash") as new evidence (e.g., "Inflation data") becomes available.
MATH 17. Prior vs Posterior probability ⭐
+
Prior: Belief before seeing data.
Posterior: Belief updated after seeing data.
🤖 Trading Algorithms (Super Important)
ALGO 18. What is Algorithmic Trading ⭐
+
The use of computer programs to execute trades based on a defined set of instructions (price, timing, or volume) at speeds impossible for a human.
ALGO 19. Advantages of Algorithmic Trading
+
Speed, accuracy, reduced emotion, ability to backtest, and lower transaction costs due to efficient execution.
ALGO 20. What is Backtesting ⭐
+
Running a trading strategy against historical data to see how it would have performed before live trading. It helps estimate risk, consistency, and robustness.
ALGO 21. Steps to develop trading strategy ⭐
+
1. Strategy Identification. 2. Backtesting. 3. Risk Management. 4. Execution System Setup. 5. Optimization.
ALGO 22. SMA-based trading strategy ⭐
+
Uses Simple Moving Averages. A common strategy is the "Crossover": Buy when a short-term SMA (e.g., 50-day) crosses above a long-term SMA (e.g., 200-day).
ALGO 23. Intraday vs Daily trading strategy ⭐
+
Intraday: Closing all positions before the market closes (avoids overnight risk).
Daily: Holding positions over multiple days to catch larger trends.
RISK 24. Risk Management in trading ⭐
+
The process of protecting your capital. It involves position sizing (how much to buy) and exit strategies.
RISK 25. Stop Loss, Trailing Stop, Take Profit ⭐
+
Stop Loss: Auto-sell if price drops to X to limit loss.
Trailing Stop: A stop-loss that moves UP with the price (locks in profit).
Take Profit: Auto-sell once target profit is reached.
ALGO 26. Performance evaluation of trading algorithm ⭐
+
Evaluated using Sharpe Ratio, Maximum Drawdown (biggest peak-to-trough drop), and Win/Loss Ratio.
🚨 Fraud Analytics (V. High Prob)
FRAUD 27. What is Fraud Detection using ML ⭐
+
Using historical data to train models that can distinguish between legitimate and fraudulent transactions in milliseconds.
FRAUD 28. What is Anomaly Detection ⭐
+
Finding patterns in data that do not conform to expected behavior (Outliers). E.g., A massive transaction in a foreign country after years of local small spends.
FRAUD 29. Supervised vs Unsupervised fraud detection ⭐
+
Supervised: Model learns from "Labeled" data (Known Fraud vs. Known Legitimate).
Unsupervised: Model finds "unusual" behavior without being told what fraud looks like (Clustering).
FRAUD 30. Imbalanced Data problem ⭐
+
In banking, 99.9% of transactions are legitimate and only 0.1% are fraud. A model might just guess "Legitimate" every time and be 99.9% accurate but still fail to catch any fraud.
FRAUD 31. ML algorithms used in fraud detection ⭐
+
Random Forest, XGBoost, Isolation Forest, and Deep Learning (Autoencoders).
FRAUD 32. Steps to build fraud detection system ⭐
+
1. Data Collection. 2. Feature Engineering. 3. Handling Imbalance (SMOTE). 4. Model Training. 5. Evaluation (Recall/Precision).
FRAUD 33. Visualization of fraud patterns
+
Network graphs (showing linked accounts), Heatmaps (location of fraud), and Time-series plots (spikes in activity).
🧠 Mixed / Viva Killers
VIVA 34. What is Overfitting ⭐
+
When a model performs perfectly on training data but fails on new data because it memorized the noise instead of the pattern.
VIVA 35. Model evaluation (Accuracy, Precision, Recall) ⭐
+
  • Accuracy: Total correct / Total. (Useless for fraud).
  • Precision: Of all predicted frauds, how many were real?
  • Recall: Of all real frauds, how many did we catch? (Most important for banking!)
