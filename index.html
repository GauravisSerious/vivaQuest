📊 Data & Social Media Analytics

Complete Practicals + Theory Viva Guide
Practicals 1 & 2: Platforms, Tools & Data Collection
Pract 1 Q1: What are specific Engagement Metrics observed at the Page, Post, and Member levels? HOT
+
  • Page-Level: Total Followers, Overall Reach, Follower Growth Rate, Total Mentions. Measures brand health.
  • Post-Level: Likes, Comments, Shares, Retweets, Video Views, Click-Through Rate (CTR). Measures content success.
  • Member-Level: User Demographics (Age, Location), Top Fans, Influencer interactions. Measures audience composition.
Pract 1 Q2: How do Facebook Insights and Google Analytics differ when dealing with Social Media Analytics?
+

Facebook Insights provides native, in-platform data on your specific FB page (demographics of fans, reach per post, native engagement).

Google Analytics tracks how much of your website's traffic specifically orginated from your social media platforms (referral traffic). It tracks if a user clicked a Twitter link and then actually bought a product (Conversion Tracking).

Pract 2 Q3: In Data Collection, what is the clear difference between Scraping, Crawling, and APIs? V.IMP
+
  • Crawling: Automated bots systematically navigating website hyperlinks to map out pages (like Google Search).
  • Scraping: A programmatic method (using Python's BeautifulSoup or Selenium) to extract visible HTML text from a webpage structure. Prone to breaking when UI changes.
  • API (Application Programming Interface): The official, platform-sanctioned way to request data. APIs return clean, structured data (typically in JSON format) without needing to parse messy HTML. Example: Twitter Developer API.
Practicals 3 & 4: Cleaning, Storage, EDA & Visualization
Pract 3 Q4: Why is NoSQL (MongoDB) heavily utilized for Social Media Data instead of traditional SQL?
+

Because social media data is vastly unstructured or semi-structured. A single tweet might contain text, 3 images, 5 hashtags, and location data, while another might only contain text. SQL requires rigid tables, while MongoDB (NoSQL) utilizes schema-less BSON document storage, making it perfectly suited for highly variable, nested social media data.

Pract 3 Q5: What are the primary preprocessing steps in text data cleaning using Python? HOT
+

Using libraries like `NLTK` or `spaCy`, the pipeline usually involves:

  1. Tokenization: Splitting massive text arrays into isolated words.
  2. Lowercasing: Ensuring uniformity (so "Hello" = "hello").
  3. Stop-Word Removal: Deleting low-value structural words ("the", "is", "at").
  4. Noise Removal: Using Regular Expressions (Regex) to delete URLs, @mentions, and HTML tags.
  5. Stemming/Lemmatization: Converting verbs/plurals to their root form ("jumping" -> "jump").
Pract 4 Q6: What is the goal of EDA (Exploratory Data Analysis) in Social Media?
+

EDA is the initial, non-predictive stage of analysis. Its goal is to uncover raw patterns, spot anomalies, and check data assumptions. Forms of visualization in EDA include:

  • Word Clouds: Visually showing the most prominent words in a dataset.
  • Bar Charts/Histograms: For viewing sentiment distributions (e.g. 600 positive tweets vs 200 negative).
  • Time-Series Line Graphs: To visualize hashtag trending volume over a month.
Practicals 5 & 6: Content & Structure Models
Pract 5 Q7: Explain Sentiment/Opinion Analysis and the tools used. HOT
+

Sentiment Analysis falls under Content-Based Analysis. It utilizes NLP to classify user text (reviews/comments) as Positive, Negative, or Neutral.

  • Tools used: VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner) works excellently for social media as it understands emojis, slang, and capitalization (e.g., "GREAT!"). Advanced models use BERT.
  • Business Application: Automatically monitoring whether an audience hates or loves a new product launch.
Pract 5 Q8: What is Topic Modeling in Content Analysis?
+

Topic Modeling is an unsupervised Machine Learning model that scans massive document sets to discover "hidden themes". If you feed it 50,000 tweets about Apple, it might cluster phrases like "Battery, Drain, Fast" into Topic 1, and "Camera, Retina, Pixel" into Topic 2.

The most algorithm used for this is Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA).

Pract 6 Q9: Define Centrality (Degree, Betweenness, Closeness) in Structure-Based Analytics. V.IMP
+

Structure-based models view social media as a graph of Nodes (Users) and Edges (Relationships/Interactions).

  • Degree Centrality: Raw number of connections. High degree = Influencer/Popular account.
  • Betweenness Centrality: A user who acts as a bridge between two completely unrelated communities (e.g., bridging Gamers and Crypto enthusiasts). They act as Information Brokers.
  • Closeness Centrality: How close a user is to everyone else on average. High closeness = Ability to spread a viral message the fastest.
Pract 6 Q10: What is Community Detection in Network Analytics?
+

It's the algorithmic process of parsing a massive social graph to find dense, isolated clusters of users that interact with each other far more frequently than the rest of the network (e.g., separating rival political camps or finding a niche fan-base).
Common algorithms used are the Louvain Method and Girvan-Newman.

Practicals 7-10: Dashboards, Promotion, & Reviews
Pract 7 Q11: How do you build a Real-Time Social Media Dashboard?
+

Unlike batch processing, real-time analytics require a streaming architecture. This involves connecting to the Twitter/X Stream API, funneling incoming JSON tweets into a message broker like Apache Kafka, and utilizing Python dashboarding frameworks like Dash (Plotly) or Streamlit to instantly update sentiment charts or keyword counters.

Pract 8 Q12: How is A/B Testing utilized in Creative Business Promotion? HOT
+

A/B testing involves running two almost identical social media ads (Content A and Content B) where only one highly specific variable is changed. For instance, comparing the exact same ad text but using a red button vs a blue button, or a video vs an image. By using analytics to see which yields a higher CTR, the business optimizes its ad budget.

Pract 9 Q13: Key metrics to track while analyzing Competitor Activities?
+

To analyze a competitor, an analyst will track:

  • Share of Voice (SOV): Finding out what percentage of total industry conversations online are about the competitor vs your brand.
  • Engagement Gap: What type of competitor posts get massive engagement?
  • Competitor Comment Sentiment: Running NLP on the competitor's user comments to identify features users complain about, thereby finding a gap your business can fill.
Pract 10 Q14: How does Text Analytics improve existing products from Customer Reviews?
+

Analyzing customer reviews relies heavily on Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA). Instead of just saying a review is "Negative", ABSA identifies exactly *what* aspect is negative.

For example, in the review "The food was amazing, but the delivery took two hours," ABSA tags [Food -> Positive] and [Logistics -> Negative]. This gives developers explicit data on what exact product service needs improving.

Theory Q15: Types of Recommendation Systems (Social Info Filtering)
+
  • Content-Based Filtering: Recommends specific posts/products purely based on the attributes/tags of things the user liked in the past.
  • Collaborative Filtering: Based on peer behavior. "Users who are similar to you and liked this product, also liked..."
  • Hybrid Filtering: Combines both text/attribute analysis and user behavior analysis to overcome the Cold Start problem.
